Piversity Certificate Program

Advanced Robotics Certification

About the Program

This certification assumed that the students have basic robotics knowledge upfront. In this certification program, students will choose one of the three streams – Robot Soccer, Robot Dance or Robot Rescue (available soon).

In each of the stream, we are not looking for students who are able to achieve champions in regional contest but acquiring the essentials for competing in RoboCup Junior competitions.

Co-created with

RoboCup Junior

RoboCup Junior (RCJ) is a division of RoboCup, a global leading not-for-profit robotics organisation. It focuses on education and aims to introduce the larger goals of the RoboCup project (creating robots) to primary and secondary school aged children (technically up through age 19). Participants compete in one of three main leagues: Soccer, Rescue or Dance.

What you will build?

Curriculum

This certification is not aiming to help students in achieving champions in regional contest but acquiring the essentials for competing in RoboCup Junior competitions. Students will choose one out of the three streams below in order to obtain the certifications.

1

Robot Soccer Stream

In this project, we will follow the latest RoboCup Junior Soccer rules. The only exception is that student could choose between using orange ball or the infra-red soccer ball.

While we don’t judge the work in terms of competition results, the soccer robot should at least include the following features:

  • Tracing soccer ball
  • Preventing own goal
  • Preventing out of bound situation
There is no limitation on sensor usage or hardware requirements, students can freely choose the strategy and implementations. However, the software and hardware design should be documented in the report.

2

Robot Dance Stream

In this project, we will follow the latest RoboCup Junior Dance rules. There are no specific requirements on the theme, robots or design. However, the performance should make use of a combinations of technologies, including sensors, motors, servo motors, lights and even stage design.
